Overall Meaning

The lyrics of Waylon Jennings' song, "Put Another Log On The Fire" are an unapologetic representation of the male entitlement that was prevalent in 1970s America. The song's narrator, who is male, demands that his partner fawn over him and cater to his every whim, from cooking him bacon and beans to fetching his slippers and boiling him tea. Meanwhile, the primary focus of the song is the singer's confusion as to why his partner is leaving him.


While the misogyny of the lyrics is undeniable, there is a hint of irony in the song as well. The singer's insistence that he is doing everything right, from warning his partner when she is "getting fat" to taking her sister driving every night, is undercut by the fact that his partner is leaving him. This suggests that, despite his efforts to control and manipulate his partner, he still cannot understand why she would want to leave him.


Ultimately, "Put Another Log On The Fire" is a powerful statement on the gender dynamics that were prevalent in the 1970s. The song's portrayal of a man who expects his partner to serve him and fulfill his every desire is a damning critique of the cultural norms that were in place at the time.
