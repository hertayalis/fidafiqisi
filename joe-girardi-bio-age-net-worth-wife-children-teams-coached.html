Joe Girardi Bio: Age, Net Worth, Wife, Children, Teams Coached

American manager and former Major League Baseball player Joe Girardi played baseball professionally.

During a major league playing career that lasted from 1989 to 2003, Girardi played catcher for the Chicago Cubs, Colorado Rockies, New York Yankees, and St. Louis Cardinals.

He was the catcher for both David Cone’s perfect game and Dwight Gooden’s no-hitter in the 1990s while playing for the Yankees, who won three World Series titles during that time.

In 2005, Girardi was hired as the Yankees’ bench manager.

He was voted National League (NL) Manager of the Year in 2006 while in charge of the Florida Marlins, but his tenure with the organization lasted just one season.

From 2008 to 2017, Girardi served as the Yankees’ manager.

His team defeated the Phillies in the 2009 World Series. Prior to being named manager of the Philadelphia Phillies in October 2019, he spent two years working as an analyst for MLB Network and Fox Sports.

Joe Girardi was fired midway through the 2022 season.

Girardi was born in Peoria, Illinois, raised in East Peoria, Illinois, and finished high school at the Spalding Institute.

In Evanston, Illinois, Girardi enrolled at Northwestern University, where he studied from 1983 to 1986.

He was a three-time Academic All-American and a two-time All-Big Ten pick while playing baseball for the Northwestern Wildcats.

All-Star (2000), 4× World Series champion (1996, 1998, 1999, 2009), and NL Manager of the Year (2006), are his notable achievements in his career, once as a professional baseball player and now as a manager.
