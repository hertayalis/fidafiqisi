Biography of Xolo Maridueña

Xolo Maridueña, born Ramario Xolo Ramirez on June 9, 2001, is an American actor known for his notable roles in television and film. He was born in Los Angeles, California, and comes from a diverse background, with Mexican, Cuban, and Ecuadorian heritage. Maridueña has four sisters, and his first name, Ramario, is a combination of the names of his maternal uncles, Ramón and Mario.

Maridueña’s career began with modeling for a Sears catalog when he was just a teenager. However, he gained widespread recognition for his role as Miguel Diaz in the popular Netflix series “Cobra Kai,” which debuted in 2018 and is still ongoing. His portrayal of Miguel Diaz, a high school student who becomes involved in karate, garnered critical acclaim and made him a fan favorite.

In addition to “Cobra Kai,” Maridueña has appeared in other television shows. He played the character Victor Graham in the NBC series “Parenthood” from 2012 to 2015. He also had guest appearances in shows such as “Major Crimes,” “Mack & Moxy,” “Rush Hour,” and “Twin Peaks.” Maridueña lent his voice to animated series like “Victor and Valentino,” “Cleopatra in Space,” and “Fast & Furious Spy Racers.”

Maridueña’s talent extends to the big screen as well. He has appeared in several films, including “Dealin’ with Idiots” in 2013, “Goodnight America” in 2020, and “#Whitina” in 2021. One of his highly anticipated projects is the superhero film “Blue Beetle,” set to be released in 2023, where he portrays the character Jaime Reyes / Blue Beetle. This role holds particular significance for Maridueña, as it marks the first time a Latino superhero is headlining his own film. He has expressed pride in being part of a project that promotes diversity and representation.

Outside of acting, Maridueña co-hosts the Lone Lobos podcast alongside Jacob Bertrand. The podcast premiered in September 2021, and the duo engages in discussions covering various topics.

In his personal life, Maridueña was romantically linked with Hannah Kepple, whom he began dating around 2019. However, the couple ended their relationship in 2021. Additionally, it has been reported that Maridueña practices the West African religion Ifá, although this information comes from an unreliable source.

Throughout his career, Maridueña has received recognition for his talent and contributions to the entertainment industry. In 2022, he won the Rising Star Impact Award at the NHMC Impact Awards for his role in “Cobra Kai.” He has also been nominated for several awards, including Imagen Awards, Young Artist Awards, and Teen Choice Awards.
